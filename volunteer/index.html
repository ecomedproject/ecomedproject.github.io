Volunteer

The EcoMed Project coordinated summer volunteer trips to Sioma, Zambia from 2007-2012. Volunteers traveled in groups of 4-6 people for 2-3 weeks at a time. The EcoMed Projectâ€™s goals are to enable and encourage pre-medical students and medical students to volunteer in the clinic and operating room, to learn about how a rural medical clinic functions, and to be exposed to the ways medicine is practiced in Zambia. The medical clinic consists of an outpatient clinic, a female and child ward, a male ward, a maternity ward, an operating room, and a lab for HIV and TB testing, among other tests. Students will rotated through various clinics in the region, including the newly built Sankande Clinic.

Daily Duties (example, subject to change)

  • 8am: Rounds in the wards, administering morning doses for all inpatients
  • 2pm: Afternoon rounds. Check inpatients, clean wounds, assess patient status
  • 9am-1pm and 2pm-5pm: Checking in outpatients including taking weight, temperature, helping the Clinical Officer diagnose and prescribe medication, acting as the pharmacists and giving patients medication
  • Cases: During outpatient hours cases vary widely and include: malaria, open wounds requiring stitches, respiratory tract infections, STDs, TB, conjunctivitis, miscarriages, worms
  • Mondays: under 5 year old clinic- children are weighed to make sure they are getting enough nutritionally and that they have all their vaccines
  • Wednesdays: Education for Moms-to-be, focus on HIV testing and prevention
  • Outreach: Any day of the week depending on diesel fuel supply and medicine supply. Drive to villages (often 2-3 hour drive) and give children de-worming medication, check pregnant females and give them supplements, weigh and measure children and update their medical card.
  • Renovations and Improvements: Help with current building projects.
